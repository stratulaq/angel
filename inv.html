Not a service company
Service on the surface.
Software underneath.

The carrier sees an operation. We are building a platform.

To the carrier, Supernova looks like a back office service.

What Supernova builds underneath is proprietary software that runs the workflow itself.

That distinction matters. Service businesses scale with headcount. Supernova uses service delivery to install software into the operation, capture the workflow, and build the infrastructure layer underneath it.

This is not a traditional service company. It is a software company using service as its wedge.

What the carrier sees
A back office that works.
Dispatch, compliance, billing, and rate negotiation handled from day one.
What is actually being built
Proprietary software behind the workflow.
Every operational step runs through systems we build and own, turning service delivery into software, data, and decision infrastructure.
Why this matters
Software economics beneath service revenue.
The operation generates revenue today, while the software captures the workflow, intelligence, and automation layer that create long-term enterprise value.
Why we win
While others wait for adoption.
Supernova is already at the Epicenter.

Most trucking software is built outside the operation and depends on carriers changing behavior to adopt it.

Supernova is the operation. It is present at the exact moment trucking happens — on every broker call, every load decision, every cash flow event that makes or breaks a carrier's week.

That gives Supernova something software-first players do not have: direct operational visibility, proprietary execution data, and the ability to automate from within the workflow instead of selling into it from the outside.

This advantage compounds with every load handled.

01 We operate at the point of execution. Not after the fact. In the live workflow where dispatch decisions, broker interactions, and carrier outcomes actually happen.
02 Every load creates proprietary operating data. Pricing behavior, broker responsiveness, carrier reliability, exception patterns, and cash flow timing — generated through execution, not assumptions.
03 Execution data becomes automation advantage. Because we run the workflow ourselves, we see what should be standardized, assisted, and automated first — and we can ship against real operational truth.
04 The moat compounds with scale. More carriers and more loads improve the workflow, deepen the data layer, and increase switching friction. Competitors can build software, but they cannot recreate execution history.
